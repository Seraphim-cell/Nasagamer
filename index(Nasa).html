The 12,600 kg Challenge: Martian Circular Economy

During a hypothetical three-year mission to Mars and back, an eight-person crew would accumulate **12,600 kg** of inorganic waste, including packaging, textiles, and structural materials. This is an unacceptable waste of mass and a critical supply chain failure.

Our solution focuses on creating a **Martian Circular Economy** where trash is instantly diverted and converted into mission-critical resources like **3D printer filament**, **radiation shielding tiles**, and **habitat composites**, securing human presence for future deep space exploration.

Phase 0: Arrival and Initial Habitat Setup (Months 0–1)

The first critical weeks focus on survival and setup. Waste processing systems are offline, making **temporary storage and creative reuse** the primary protocols.

INITIAL DEPLOYMENT AND WASTE COLLECTION PROTOCOLS

Activities & Generated Waste

  • **Landing & Unpacking:** Generates high-volume, low-mass foam packaging, protective wraps, and cube-frame structure components.
  • **Immediate Habitat Setup:** Scraps of plastic and metal clips from structural adjustments.

Waste Diversion & Reuse Strategy

  • **Foam Packaging:** Reused immediately as **temporary padding**, **insulation panels**, and **soft storage containers** until the TCPS unit is powered up.
  • **Plastic Wrapping / Tarps:** Repurposed as **dust shields** for sensitive equipment or temporary floor liners.
  • **3-D Cube Frame:** Reused as a rigid, temporary support structure for internal instruments.

Key Processing Principles

The objective is **volume control** and **contamination prevention**. All waste is manually sorted into segregated, sealed containers, prioritizing immediate functional reuse over long-term processing.

Phase 1: Early Surface Operations (Months 1–4)

The TCPS compactor and basic sterilizer are partially online. The mission shifts to processing the bulk waste accumulated during Phase 0 and managing daily consumables.

TCPS COMPACTION AND POLYMER SORTING ACTIVATED

Activities & Generated Waste

  • **Consumables:** Daily meal packaging (multilayer films, drink pouches), hygiene wipes, paper products.
  • **Habitat Ops:** Used textiles (towels, clothing), filters from water/air recovery systems.

Waste Diversion & Reuse Strategy

  • **Clean Plastics (Polyethylene, etc.):** Sorted, washed, and prepped for the **Refabricator** (3D printing filament).
  • **Contaminated Films / Foams:** Compressed by the TCPS into **dense, inert waste tiles** for immediate radiation shielding.
  • **Textiles (Clothing/Wipes):** Sterilized, cut into straps or cleaning rags, or shredded for use in **composite filler**.

Key Processing Principles

Establish **sorting discipline** (clean vs. dirty) and prioritize **volume reduction** using the TCPS. The goal is to stabilize and repurpose low-value, high-volume materials into structural assets (tiles).

Phase 2: Normal Operations (Months 4–19)

The **full Material Reclamation System** (Pyrolysis, Induction Melting, Refabricator, TCPS) is operational. This is the period of peak recycling efficiency and resource creation.

CLOSED-LOOP RECLAMATION SYSTEMS AT PEAK EFFICIENCY

Activities & Generated Waste

  • **Scientific:** Lab plastics, experimental packaging, glassware.
  • **Maintenance:** Aluminum components, fasteners, worn-out composite scraps from repairs.

Waste Diversion & Reuse Strategy

  • **Aluminum / Metals:** Melted using the **induction foundry** and cast into structural components or fed to a metallic 3D printer.
  • **Mixed/Complex Polymers:** Processed via **pyrolysis** (thermal breakdown) to recover base hydrocarbons for fuels, lubricants, or energy.
  • **Composite Scraps:** Shredded and combined with local regolith to create reinforced **regolith-polymer panels**.

Key Processing Principles

Focus shifts to **value extraction**. Polymers are maximized as **filament** (AM feedstock) or converted to **fuel** (energy/mobility). Metals are reclaimed for high-strength application.

Phase 3: Birthday Celebration (Month 20)

A morale event demonstrating the ability to **repurpose** materials for social and psychological benefit, ensuring minimal waste generation during a non-essential activity.

REPURPOSING FOR HABITAT AND MORALE BOOST

Activities & Generated Waste

  • **Decoration Prep:** Textiles and food packaging (cut up for streamers, party hats, small banners).
  • **Post-Event:** Used temporary decorations; clean food packaging.

Waste Diversion & Reuse Strategy

  • **Temporary Décor:** Textiles/packaging used for decorations are immediately returned to the system: shredded for insulation or processed for filament/tiles.
  • **Clean Food Packaging:** Recycled into small, custom-printed **storage containers** or **insulation mats** using the Refabricator.

Key Processing Principles

Maximize **circulatory** in non-essential use cases. Every item used for celebration must have a predefined, scheduled return path to the recycling or structural system.

Phase 4: Scientific Discovery (Month 23)

Management of waste and byproducts from advanced experimental units, particularly the CO₂-to-O₂ conversion apparatus.

BYPRODUCT MANAGEMENT: CARBON AND REGOLITH INTERFACE

Activities & Generated Waste

  • **Experiment Ops:** Filters, mesh, collection apparatus from atmosphere conversion.
  • **EVA:** Used EVA gloves and dust covers (often highly contaminated with regolith).

Waste Diversion & Reuse Strategy

  • **Surplus Carbon:** The byproduct of CO₂ conversion is repurposed as **soil enrichment** for bioregenerative systems, or as **filler** to enhance regolith-polymer composite strength.
  • **EVA/Contaminated Gear:** Dedusted, sterilized, and shredded. The material is permanently compacted into **TCPS shielding tiles** due to regolith contamination.

Key Processing Principles

Focus on **byproduct valorization** (turning waste of one system into an input for another). Aggressively manage **regolith-contaminated waste** by encapsulation (TCPS) to minimize habitat exposure.

Phase 5: Routine Operations and Final Year (Months 24–36)

The mission maintains peak recycling efficiency while initiating the systematic **deconstruction and site cleanup** for future operations.

END-OF-MISSION DECONSTRUCTION AND RESOURCE PRESERVATION

Activities & Generated Waste

  • **Deconstruction:** Dismantling temporary habitat modules, packaging components for Earth return, and final sweep of the site.
  • **Routine:** Final high-volume stream of food packaging and hygiene products.

Waste Diversion & Reuse Strategy

  • **All High-Value Components (Aluminum, Clean Composites):** Sorted, labeled, and placed in designated storage for the next mission.
  • **Low-Value Waste (Remaining Packaging):** Compressed into TCPS tiles, which are then used as part of the **landing pad base** or **future habitat foundation**.
  • **Site Cleanliness:** Foam and bubble wrap are completely compacted or repurposed. **The Mars site is left clear** of non-essential materials.

Key Processing Principles

**Legacy Management:** Ensure a clean departure by processing all remaining trash into a stable, useful state for the next crew. **The only mass left behind is useful mass.**
