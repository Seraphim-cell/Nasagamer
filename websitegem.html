The 12,600 kg Challenge: Martian Circular Economy

During a hypothetical three-year mission to Mars and back, an eight-person crew would accumulate **12,600 kg** of inorganic waste, including packaging, textiles, and structural materials. This scenario creates a pressing need to recycle available materials, rather than execute the expensive and inefficient processes of transporting additional resources from Earth.

Our challenge is to design sustainable systems that manage, reuse, or recycle this inorganic waste, turning trash into valuable resources like **3D printer filament**, **radiation shielding tiles**, and **habitat composites**, preparing humanity for future exploration of unknown worlds.

Phase 0: Arrival and Inflatable Habitat Setup (Months 0–1)

Crew lands on Mars; habitat setup is critical. Waste management systems arrive two days later, so initial waste handling is limited.

Activities Generating Waste

  • Landing & Unpacking: Foam packaging, bubble wrap, air cushions, protective wraps around habitat/cube frame.
  • Habitat Setup: Scraps of foam, plastic, metal clips from adjustments.
  • Temporary Work Area: Protective coverings and gloves used for handling equipment.

Waste Types & Processing / Temporary Use

  • Foam Packaging: Reuse as temporary padding, insulation, storage bins, or small structural supports.
  • 3-D Cube Frame: Reused after inflation as a temporary support structure for instruments or workstations.
  • Plastic Wrapping / Tarps: Reuse as dust shields, temporary floor lining, or protective covers.
  • Cardboard / Rocket Packaging: Flatten and store for temporary insulation or makeshift tables.

Key Processing Principles

No active recycling unit yet – all waste must be temporarily stored or **creatively reused**.

Examples: Foam & bubble wrap $\rightarrow$ insulation; 3-D cube frame $\rightarrow$ experimental structures. **Minimal waste exposure** and **temporary containment** are critical.

Phase 1: Early Surface Operations (Months 1–4)

Crew settles in; waste management systems (TCPS compactor, basic sterilizer) are partially online. Focus shifts to daily crew activity waste.

[ Placeholder for Phase 1 Image ]

Activities Generating Waste

  • Habitat Operations: Daily use of clothing, towels, hygiene wipes; office supplies.
  • Food & Consumables: Daily meals generate multilayer packaging, drink pouches.
  • Waste System Startup: Filters and wipes from testing water/air recovery systems; protective gloves.

Waste Types & Processing / Temporary Use

  • Clothing / Textiles: Sterilize $\rightarrow$ cut/patch into straps, rags, insulation; shred for composites.
  • Paper & Office Supplies: Reuse as notes; shred to wipes/fiberboard; compact in TCPS tiles.
  • Food Packaging: Wash/sterilize $\rightarrow$ sort clean plastics for **Refabricator filament**; compact contaminated films into **TCPS tiles**.
  • EVA Expendables: Dedust $\rightarrow$ reuse polymeric parts; compact worn/contaminated in **TCPS**.

Key Processing Principles

Early emphasis on reuse & sterilization (textiles, wipes). Volume management via TCPS (foams, packaging $\rightarrow$ tiles for shielding). Sorting priority (Clean polymers $\rightarrow$ Refabricator; dirty $\rightarrow$ compaction).

Phase 2: Normal Operations (Months 4–19)

The **full waste management system** (TCPS, sterilizer, Refabricator, limited pyrolysis) is operational. Sustainability focus: maximize recycling efficiency.

[ Placeholder for Phase 2 Image ]

Activities Generating Waste

  • Scientific & Maintenance: Lab experiments (packaging, plastics, glass), repairs (fasteners, composite scraps).
  • Crew Life: Daily food consumption, clothing/wipes cycling out.
  • EVA Operations: Regular sorties generate used glove liners, dust covers, regolith-contaminated waste.

Waste Types & Processing / Reuse

  • Food Packaging: Clean plastics $\rightarrow$ **Filament**; multilayer/contaminated $\rightarrow$ **TCPS tiles** or **pyrolysis** for gas/fuel.
  • Aluminum / Composites: Salvaged. Aluminum is fed to **induction melting**; composite scraps embedded in **regolith–polymer panels**.
  • EVA Gloves & Expendables: Dedust $\rightarrow$ sterilize/reuse; compact regolith-contaminated in **TCPS**.

Key Processing Principles

Closed-loop recycling baseline for polymer, textile, metal. **TCPS tiles** serve dual roles (waste stabilization & construction). **Material Reclamation** (metals, clean polymers) for **Additive Manufacturing (AM)**.

Phase 3: Birthday Celebration (Month 20)

Objective: boost morale while demonstrating the versatility and sustainability of the habitat recycling system using only existing materials.

[ Placeholder for Phase 3 Image ]

Activities Generating Waste (Post-Celebration)

  • Decoration Preparation: Clothing/washcloths cut for decorations, plastic wraps/pouches for hats/banners, food packaging for containers/confetti.

Waste Types & Processing / Reuse

  • Clothing / Washcloths: Repurposed as streamers/décor; post-event, shredded into cleaning rags or insulation.
  • Plastic Wraps / Thermal Pouches: Shaped into party hats/banners; post-event scraps fed into **TCPS** or used as padding.
  • Food Packaging: Cleaned and recycled into storage containers, insulation mats, or filament via **Refabricator**.

Key Processing Principles

Full repurposing priority: Every decoration element originates from already-used material. Temporary functional use followed by immediate reintegration. **Minimal additional waste** generated.

Phase 4: Scientific Discovery (Month 23)

Crew conducts CO₂-to-oxygen conversion experiments. Objective: maximize reuse of experimental waste and integrate byproducts into habitat operations.

[ Placeholder for Phase 4 Image ]

Activities Generating Waste

  • Experiment Operations: EVA tasks for CO₂-to-O₂ units, manipulation of apparatus (filters, mesh, carbon collection), sample collection.

Waste Types & Processing / Reuse

  • Surplus Carbon: Byproduct of CO₂-to-O₂ conversion. Used for **soil enrichment**, **thermal insulation**, or **regolith-polymer composite printing filler**.
  • EVA Gloves & Protective Equipment: Sterilized for reuse; degraded $\rightarrow$ shredded for **composite filler**.
  • Filters / Lab Tools: Cleaned, regenerated, or repurposed; damaged items disassembled for components.

Key Processing Principles

Maximize experimental byproduct reuse (Surplus Carbon). Sterilization first for EVA gloves, fabrics, instruments. **Closed-loop integration** of experimental waste into habitat systems.

Phase 5: Routine Operations and Final Year (Months 24–36)

Maintain sustainable operations, process waste efficiently, and prepare for **end-of-mission deconstruction**.

[ Placeholder for Phase 5 Image ]

Activities Generating Waste

  • Routine Operations: Daily meals, cleaning, experiments generate food packaging, fabrics, hygiene items.
  • End-of-Mission Preparations (Month 36): Dismantling habitat modules; sorting, compacting, preparing materials for transport or future mission reuse.

Waste Types & Processing / Reuse

  • Food Packaging: Shredded and pressed into storage containers/insulation mats; high-volume packaging $\rightarrow$ **thermal processing (TtSG)** for energy.
  • Aluminum / Composites: Sorted, compacted, salvaged for reuse or **AM feedstock**; metals processed via **induction foundry**.
  • Site Cleanliness: Foam and bubble wrap are either transported, compacted, or repurposed. Mars site is left clear.

Key Processing Principles

Closed-loop management continues. **Material prioritization:** High-value materials reclaimed first. **End-of-mission preparation:** All remaining materials sorted for transport, storage, or reuse in subsequent missions.
